Through comprehensive analysis and mining of both structured and unstructured data, we efficiently generate targeted drug candidates and identify promising compounds.

Targeted Drug Design

Targeted drug design develops molecules that interact with biological targets like proteins or enzymes for therapeutic effects. To overcome challenges like low synthetic accessibility, we created a fragment-based ligand design algorithm. This approach integrates active fragment generation and molecular reconstruction, using ordered fragmentation and reconstruction to train sequence-to-sequence and language models. Our model incorporates target contribution perception and a Bag-of-Words classifier, enhancing protein binding site awareness and interpretability. Pretrained on large-scale datasets, it excels in binding affinity, drug-likeness, and synthetic accessibility. A simulated annealing algorithm fine-tunes molecular properties, improving efficiency and reducing drug development costs.

Drug Response

Drug response prediction is key to personalized medicine, identifying cell reactions to treatments. To improve accuracy, we propose a gene expression-based model using self-supervised learning to integrate gene expression data, gene interaction networks, and molecular structures. This enhances drug sensitivity predictions and supports clinical applications, advancing personalized treatment strategies.

Covalent Drug Design

Covalent drug design develops irreversible inhibitors that form strong bonds with target proteins. CovaGEN, a deep generative method using a reinforced latent diffusion model, designs drug molecules with high binding affinity, drug-likeness, safety, and covalent binding ability. Pre-trained on millions of drug-like molecules, it ensures chemical validity and synthetic accessibility. It integrates protein sequence encoding for target affinity and a covalent warhead-guided mechanism for binding. Reinforcement learning optimizes safety, reducing toxicity by 12% while improving drug-likeness by 31% and synthetic accessibility by 7%. For EGFR T790M and SARS-CoV-2 Mpro, over half of the designed inhibitors show strong binding potential, highlighting CovaGEN’s value.
